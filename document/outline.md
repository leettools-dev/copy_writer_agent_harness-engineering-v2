LLM Harness Engineering: Market and Breakpoint Analysis

Purpose: Produce an evidence-backed, founder-focused report defining LLM harness engineering, mapping the market, analyzing technical and commercial bottlenecks, and identifying top newcomer wedges.

Section plans:

01 Executive summary
- Purpose: Give a concise decision memo for founders, summarizing definition, current state, top providers, biggest gaps, top 3 wedges, and recommended wedge.
- Key question: Where can a small startup make a credible entry and why?
- Evidence plan: Use primary sources for provider claims (websites, GH metrics), cite VC/market maps for structure.
- Table/figure: short provider table (top 8 players) with categories and traction signals.

02 Market map and taxonomy
- Purpose: Define LLM harness engineering and present a clear taxonomy that distinguishes infra, developer tooling, platform tooling, and enterprise ops.
- Key question: What are the meaningful boundaries in the market?
- Evidence plan: Use b2venture taxonomy as starting point, triangulate with other market maps (a16z, VC pieces), and primary vendor docs.
- Table: taxonomy table mapping categories to examples and deployment models.
- Illustration: layered stack diagram (stage 1-3) - conceptual SVG.

03 Provider landscape
- Purpose: Detailed vendor analysis organized by category (both commercial and OSS).
- Key question: Which providers are practically important and why?
- Evidence plan: For each provider gather product pages, docs, pricing, GH metrics (if OSS), funding/press mentions.
- Table: provider comparison matrix (abstraction level, deployment, eval, obs, enterprise readiness, traction).

04 Customer and JTBD analysis
- Purpose: Identify key personas and their core jobs-to-be-done.
- Key question: What problems do buyers pay to solve and what would make them switch?
- Evidence plan: Cite engineering blog posts, case studies, hiring posts (LLM Ops), and community signals.
- Table: persona -> JTBD -> current tools -> pain points.

05 Technical bottlenecks
- Purpose: Classify technical problems as solved/partially solved/unsolved with evidence.
- Key question: Which technical problems are gating adoption?
- Evidence plan: Use OSS issues, blog posts, conference talks, and academic papers (where relevant).
- Table: problem -> status -> evidence -> implication for product design.

06 Commercial landscape and moat analysis
- Purpose: Assess defensibility and monetization across layers.
- Key question: Where does value stick and where will it be commoditized?
- Evidence plan: Funding data, partnership announcements, product PLG signals, enterprise integrations.
- Table: layer -> defensibility -> monetization dynamics.

07 Breakpoint analysis and ranked wedges
- Purpose: Propose and rank newcomer wedges with rigorous evidence and GTM assessment.
- Key question: Which wedge has best risk-adjusted chance to win?
- Evidence plan: Cross-reference product pain points, vendor gaps, buyer willingness to pay, technical feasibility.
- Table: wedge -> buyer -> urgent pain -> why incumbents fail -> feasibility -> risk.

08 Strategic recommendation
- Purpose: Operational advice for a startup: MVP, GTM, integrations, roadmap, metrics.
- Key question: What to build first and how to prove PMF?
- Evidence plan: Use chosen wedge, persona, and traction signals to recommend early metrics and integrations.
- Table: 6-month roadmap with milestones and success metrics.

09 Appendix
- Purpose: Source list, company table, comparison matrices, terminology notes.
- Evidence plan: Maintain a references list with URLs and short notes.
